Quality & compliance

How we talk about pharmacies, prescribing, documentation, and refunds — aligned with what you’ll see in checkout and in our Terms.

503B outsourcing facility (peptides)

Many peptide preparations discussed on this site are supplied through outsourcing facilities operating under section 503B of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act when that is what your prescription and pharmacy routing require. Your physician and dispensing pharmacy determine the exact sourcing path; we describe the model here so expectations stay transparent.

Compounded medications are not FDA-approved in the same way as mass-manufactured drugs. Labels and release testing still follow applicable pharmacy rules for the dosage form you are prescribed.

503A patient-specific compounding (GLP-1)

GLP-1 programs (for example semaglutide and tirzepatide) are typically fulfilled as patient-specific prescriptions compounded under 503A when that is what your clinician and pharmacy select.

We repeat the same disclosure used across the site: compounded medications are not FDA-approved; they are prepared for an identified patient when a physician determines a prescription is appropriate.

Physician-prescribed via good faith exam

Enrollment flows use Qualiphy for telehealth and good-faith exam documentation before prescriptions are issued. Authentic Wellness facilitates access; independent licensed physicians make medical decisions.

Nothing on this page replaces your after-visit summary, prescription labeling, or direct guidance from your clinician.

Third-party tested with COAs on request

We describe sourcing as rigorous and testing-backed because pharmacy partners operate under formal quality systems; certificates of analysis may be available on request depending on product and pharmacy policy.

If you need documentation for a specific shipment, route the request through your care team so it can be coordinated with the dispensing pharmacy.

Full refund if not cleared

If your independent physician does not approve medication, medication-related enrollment fees are refunded in full. Consultation fees are non-refundable, consistent with the homepage and Terms of Service.

Once medication has shipped, returns are generally limited by pharmacy handling rules; see Terms for the complete policy.
